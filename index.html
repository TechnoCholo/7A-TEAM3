Bones are important because they support our body, protect our organs, and help us move. They also store minerals like calcium and produce blood cells inside the bone marrow.

Types of Bones

The human body has different types of bones. Long bones help with movement, short bones give support, flat bones protect organs, and irregular bones have special shapes.

Example: The femur is a long bone.

Functions of Bones

Bones have many important functions. They support the body, protect organs, help movement, store minerals, and produce blood cells.

Example: The ribs protect the heart and lungs.

Major Bones

Some major bones in the human body are the skull, spine, ribs, pelvis, femur, tibia, humerus, radius, and ulna.

Fact: The femur is the longest bone in the body.

Diseases and Injuries

Bones can be affected by diseases and injuries. A fracture is a broken bone. Osteoporosis is a disease that makes bones weak and easier to break. Bones are living parts of the body. They grow, repair themselves, and help the body stay healthy.

Examples: Osteogenesis Imperfecta, Spiral fracture

Fun Facts

1. The adult human body has 206 bones, but babies are born with around 270 bones. Many fuse together as they grow. 2. The smallest bone in the body is the stapes, located in the ear, and it is only about 3 mm long. 3. Bones are living tissue and constantly rebuild themselves. About every 10 years, you basically have a “new” skeleton. 4. Bone marrow inside certain bones produces millions of blood cells every second. 5. Teeth are not considered bones, even though they look similar and contain calcium.
